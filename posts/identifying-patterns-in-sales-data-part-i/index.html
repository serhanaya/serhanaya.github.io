Introduction

In this first part, the objective is to make sense of the data and then figure out what questions to ask and how to frame them, as well as how best to manipulate the data to get the answers. Here is some information on the data set.

This data set contains article data for four products for the year 2017 in Germany. The columns of the data set are labeled as:

     'rmm13', 'cdm1', 'cwm1', 'cmm1', 'cmm2', 'cmm13', 'mpdm1', 'mpwm1',
     'mpmm1', 'mpmm2', 'mpmm13', 'stock_level', 'retail_price']

Each row of the data set is an entry for a product_id on a given calendar_date.

For each product_id and calendar_date, the columns volume_sold, revenue, cost, stock_level and retail_price are defined as follows:

1
2
3
4
5
6

    volume_sold: the number of units sold on that calendar_date
    revenue: the revenue generated expressed in the local currency on that calendar_date
    cost: the cost of the units sold expressed in the local currency on that calendar_date
    stock_level: the number of units of stock at the end of that calendar_date
    retail_price: the mean retail_price of that product_id on that calendar_date

The other columns are defined as follows:

1
2
3
4
5

    vdm1: volume sold on the day before (dm1 = day minus 1)
    vwm1: volume sold in the past 7 days (wm1 = week minus 1)
    vmm1: volume sold in the last 28 days (mm1 = month minus 1)
    vmm2: volume sold between 29 and 56 days ago (mm2 = month minus 2)
    vmm13: volume sold last yearfor 28 days on the same date (mm13 = month minus 13)

The same abbreviation format is used for revenue, cost and maximum price for various time periods before the given calendar date. In particular,

1
2
3
4

    rdm1: revenue on the day before 
    rwm1, rmm1, rmm2, rmm13: revenue at various time periods before the given calendar_date
    cdm1, cwm1, cmm1, cmm2, cmm13: cost of the article at various time periods before the given calendar_date
    mpdm1, mpwm1, mpmm1, mpmm2, mpmm13: the maximum retail price at various time periods before the given calendar_date

We have reason to believe that there exists a functional relationship between the volume sold and some or all the remaining features (including perhaps additional features created from the existing ones). In the second part of this study, we will create a model that predicts the volume_sold. We will talk about the proposed model, performance metrics and which features are utilized.

Exploratory Data Analysis

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is the first step in the data analysis process. The goal is to make sense of the data we have and then figure out what questions we want to ask and how to frame them, as well as how best to manipulate the data to get the answers we need.

We do this by taking a broad look at patterns, trends, outliers, unexpected results and so on in the data, using visual and quantitative methods to get a sense of the story this tells.

Effect of stock_level on volume_sold

The day after out of stock. Since stock_level column is the number of units of stock at the end of that calendar_date, I checked the items sold for each product the day after the date when stock_level=0. For product 26104 it turned out to be a great indicator for the items to be sold that day. Looking at all the products, the mean of the items sold for the the day after the date when stock_level=0 are less than the mean of the items sold for other times:

def add_after_stock0(df, product_id):
    """
    Adds after_stock0 column to the dataframe and removes the stock_level column. 
    returns: dataframe
    """
    # 
    df_product = df.loc[df['product_id'] == int(product_id)]
    df_product['after_stock0'] = 0
    # 
    day_after_out_of_stock = get_date_after_stock0(df, product_id)
    # 
    df_product.loc[df_product['calendar_date'].isin(day_after_out_of_stock), 'after_stock0'] = 1
    df_product.drop('stock_level', axis='columns', inplace=True)
    
    return df_product

Summary

  • Selection:
    • revenue (and related columns), cost (and related columns) and woy (since we only have 1 year of data) columns are removed.
  • Filtering:
    • Rows including negative values are removed.
    • An extreme value in volume_sold column for the product 158105 is detected and removed.
  • Missing data:
    • Some missing data in product 26104 causes gaps in vol_sold_26104 columns in other dataframes. Also shifted vol_sold_<product_id> columns have missing values at first row. For linear interpolation .interpolation() method is applied with (limit_direction='both') to fill the consecutive NaNs in both direction.
  • Feature extraction
    • The day after the date stock_level=0 is represented with the column after_stock0.
    • When predicting the volume_sold for each product, the volume_sold for other products will be used as a feature.
