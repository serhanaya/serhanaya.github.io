Guided Random Search Methods - Part I

Posted Feb 1, 2016 2016-02-01T07:00:00+02:00 by S. Aya

Gradient-based methods often have to be restarted to ensure that the local optimum reached is indeed the global one. In this post, we explore three different types of solution techniques that do not require the objective function to be either continuous or differentible. The solution techniques are:

1
2
3

* Genetic Algorithms (GA)
* Simulated Annealing (SA)
* Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO)

Genetic algorithms

GA mimics the genetic process in which hereditary characteristics are transmitted from a parent to an offspring. The variables are coded into binary string. In the reproduction step, the best individuals in the population are selected for mating. The diversity in the population is created using crossover and mutation operations. The mutation operator changes the bit 1 to 0 or vice versa with a small probability.

Table 1. Genetic algorithm
Step 1: Objective function \(f(x), x=\left(x_1,\text{...},x_d\right){}^T\)
Step 2: Encode the solutions into chromosomes (strings)
Step 3: Define fitness \(F\) (eg, \(F \propto\) \(f(x)\) for maximization)
Step 4: Generate the initial population
Step 5: Initialize the probabilities of crossover \((p_c)\) and mutation \((p_m)\)
Step 6: while ( \(t <\) Max number of generations )
Generate new solution by crossover and mutation
Crossover with a crossover probability \(p_c\)
Mutate with a mutation probability \(p_m\)
Accept the new solutions if their fitness increase
Select the current best for the next generation (elitism)
Update \( t = t + 1\)
end while
Step 7: Decode the results and visualization

Simulated annealing

Simulated annealing is an optimization technique that has derived its name from the process of annealing of solids, in which the solid is heated and the allowed to cool slowly until its molecules reach a minimum energy state.

Table 2. Algorithm for Simulated Annealing
Step 1: Initialize \(\varepsilon\) variable boundaries \(x_{i,\min}\) and \(x_{i,\max}\)
Step 2: Compute stating value of the variables as \(x_{i} = x_{i,\min} + (x_{i,\max} - x_{i,\min}) u_i\) 
Step 3: Compute \(E_{old} = f(x_i)\)
Step 4: Compute \(\Delta x_{i+1} = \varepsilon x_i u_i\) and \(x_{i+1} = x_i + \Delta x_i\)
Step 5: Compute \( E_{new} = f(x_{i+1})\)
Step 6: If \(E_{\text{new}}<E_{\text{old}}\)
then \(E_{\text{old}}=E_{\text{new}}\)
else if \( e^{-\frac{E_{\text{new}}-E_{\text{old}}}{E_{\text{old}}}}>u\)
then \(E_{\text{old}}<E_{\text{new}}\)
else \( x_{i+1}=x_i-\text{$\Delta $x}_i\)
Step 7: Go to Step 4 until termination criterion is satisfied.

Particle swarm optimization

The PSO technique is inspired by the collective wisdom of a group of individuals such as a flock of birds or animals moving in herds or schools of fish moving together.

Table 3. Algorithm for Particle Swarm Optimization
Step 1: Initialize \(i_{max}, w_1, \phi_1, \phi_2, \eta, x_{i,\min}\) and \(x_{i,\max}\)
Step 2: Initialize the starting position and velocities if the variables as \(x_{i,k} = x_{i,\min} + (x_{i,\max} - x_{i,\min}) u_i\) where \(k=1...n\)
Step 3: Compute \(p_{i,k} = f(x_{i,k})\) where \(k=1...n\)
Step 4: Compute \(pbest_{i,k} = p_{i,k}\) and \(gbest_i =\) minimum\((pbest_{i,k})\)
The location of \(pbest_k\) and \(gbest\) is given by \(p_{\text{xik}}\) and \(g_{\text{ix}}\)
Step 5: Update velocity
\(\nu _{i+1,k}=\phi _2 u_i \left(g_{\text{ix}}-x_{i,k}\right)+\omega _1 \nu _{i,k}+\phi _1 u_i \left(p_{xik}-x_{i,k}\right)\)
Step 6: Update position
\(x_{i+1,k}=\nu _{i+1,k}+x_{i,k}\)
Step 7: Update fitness
\(p_{i+1,k}=f(x_{i+1,k})\)
Step 8: If \(p_{i+1,k} < pbest_{i,k}\)
then \(pbest_{i+1,k} = p_{i+1,k}\)
Step 9: Update \(gbest_{i+1} =\) minimum\((pbest_{i+1,k})\)
Step 10: If \(i < i_{max} \) then increment \(i\) and go to Step 5, else stop.

Take-aways

  • GA and PSO work with population of points in a search space whereas SA propagates through iterations with single search point.
  • SA allows points with higher objective functions to be selected with certain probability. It is often called a “hill-climbing” algorithm.
  • PSO algorithm keeps track of best position of the individual as well as that of the population in terms of the objective function.

References

  • X.S. Yang, 2014, Nature-Inspired Optimization Algorithms, Elsevier.
